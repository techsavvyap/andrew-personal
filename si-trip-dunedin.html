The road less taken

This is the Southern Scenic Route — 600 km of rugged coastline, ancient rainforest, petrified sea floors, and some of NZ's rarest wildlife. Unlike the Queenstown circuit, this route is largely tourist-free. You'll trade the après-ski crowds for yellow-eyed penguins at dusk, sea caves you can walk into at low tide, and the slow drama of the deep south — all ending at Milford Sound.

The Itinerary

Dunedin · Days 1–2
1 Sat

Fly Auckland → Dunedin · Arrive & explore the city

  • ~2hr flight, collect rental car at DUD airport
  • Dunedin's Victorian architecture — the Railway Station is one of NZ's most photographed buildings
  • Baldwin Street (world's steepest residential street), Octagon city centre
  • Dinner at one of the Princes Street restaurants
Scenic Hotel Dunedin City · 2 nights
2 Sun

Otago Peninsula — wildlife day

  • Morning: Royal Albatross Centre at Taiaroa Head — the world's only mainland albatross colony
  • Monarch Wildlife Cruise — spot albatross, little blue penguins, and fur seals from the water
  • Sandfly Bay at dusk for rare Hoiho (yellow-eyed penguins) coming ashore
🐧 Yellow-eyed penguins 🦅 Royal albatross
The Catlins · Days 3–4
3 Mon

Drive into the Catlins · Nugget Point & Cathedral Caves

  • Drive south from Dunedin (~1.5hrs to Kaka Point)
  • Nugget Point Lighthouse — rated 4.9★, one of NZ's most dramatic coastal viewpoints; fur seals on the rocks below
  • Roaring Bay bird hide — yellow-eyed penguins at dusk (one of the world's rarest penguins)
  • Cathedral Caves if the tide allows — a 30m-high sea cave system, torch required
Cathedral Caves are only accessible within ~2 hours of low tide. Check tides before heading out — the DOC website has a tide calculator.
Catlins accommodation · 1 night (Owaka or Papatowai)
4 Tue

Curio Bay, McLean Falls & on to Invercargill

  • Curio Bay — a 180-million-year-old petrified forest exposed at low tide; yellow-eyed penguins nest here too
  • Porpoise Bay — Hector's dolphins often surf the waves right in front of you
  • McLean Falls — a stunning 22m tiered waterfall (40min return walk through native bush)
  • Arrive Invercargill by mid-afternoon
🐬 Hector's dolphins 🌿 Petrified forest The Langlands Hotel, Invercargill · 1 night
Invercargill & Fiordland · Days 5–7
5 Wed

Invercargill · Drive to Te Anau

  • Morning: Invercargill's E. Hayes & Sons hardware store — famously eccentric, worth a wander
  • Optional: Bluff (30min south) — the southernmost tip of the South Island, iconic signpost
  • 2hr drive north to Te Anau through the Southland plains
  • Te Anau Glowworm Caves evening tour — book well ahead
Lakefront Lodge Te Anau · 2 nights
6 Thu

Milford Sound — the destination

  • Leave Te Anau by 7am — the drive through Fiordland is extraordinary in winter
  • Mirror Lakes, the Homer Tunnel, hanging valleys spilling with waterfalls after rain
  • 2hr scenic cruise: Mitre Peak, Stirling Falls, fur seals, and hopefully dolphins
  • Return to Te Anau by mid-afternoon; relax lakeside
August brings Milford's waterfalls at full power after winter rain — this is genuinely the best time of year to see it. Book your cruise 6–8 weeks ahead.
7 Fri

Te Anau → Dunedin → Fly home

  • Drive back to Dunedin (~3.5hrs via SH1) or drop the car in Invercargill and fly home
  • Evening flight back to Auckland from DUD (or IVC)
One-way car hire (pick up Dunedin, drop in Invercargill) can save backtracking and costs around $30–50 extra. Worth it if you prefer not to retrace the route.

How it compares to the Queenstown route

Both itineraries include Milford Sound and 7 nights for two within a $4,000 budget. The Queenstown route is more polished; this one is more raw and rewarding. Think of the Queenstown itinerary as the greatest hits — this one is the deep cut that people who've done it say was the highlight of their entire NZ trip.

04

Good to Know

Flights to Dunedin

Return AKL–DUD for two runs about $300–$450. August is actually the cheapest month to fly this route. Air New Zealand has the most flights; Jetstar is cheapest. Book around 75 days ahead for best prices.

The Catlins: no glamping

Accommodation in the Catlins is basic — motels, holiday parks, and simple B&Bs. That's part of the charm. Owaka and Papatowai are the main bases. Stock up on food in Balclutha before heading south.

Wildlife timing matters

Yellow-eyed penguins come ashore at dusk — plan your days to be at Sandfly Bay or Curio Bay by 5–6pm. The Albatross Centre and Monarch Cruise are best booked ahead. August can mean chicks are still in nests.

Cathedral Caves: check the tides

These spectacular sea caves are only accessible within ~2 hours of low tide. Check the DOC website before driving there — it would be a shame to arrive and find the entrance underwater.

One-way car hire tip

Consider picking up in Dunedin and dropping in Invercargill (~$30–50 extra). This avoids backtracking 3.5hrs and you can fly home from Invercargill or Queenstown instead, adding flexibility.

August weather down south

The Catlins and Invercargill can be cold and wet — average 8–10°C with frequent showers. Pack proper waterproofs, layers, and sturdy shoes. The upside: waterfalls everywhere, dramatic moody skies, and almost no other tourists.
